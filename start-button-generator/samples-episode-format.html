Segment One

007 & Gotham — When Licensed Games Actually Work

007 First Light — Out Now LEGO Batman: Legacy — Out Now
🔫
007 First Light launched May 27 from IO Interactive. Metacritic score: 96 — Universal Acclaim on every platform. A Bond game hasn't scored like this since GoldenEye.

Let's start with something that could have gone very wrong and instead went very right. 007 First Light is out now — IO Interactive, the studio behind the Hitman trilogy, has made a James Bond origin story that critics are calling one of the best games of the year. We're talking a Metacritic score of 96.

And then there's LEGO Batman: Legacy, which also dropped this week. Open-world Gotham, 100 Batsuits, twelve Batmobiles. Steam user reviews are sitting at 96% positive.

💬 Host note: The Bond conversation is great for generational comparison — GoldenEye N64, the Brosnan-era games, the Daniel Craig era.

★ Panel Questions — Segment 1
  • What did your relationship with James Bond games look like growing up?
  • IO Interactive built their reputation on patience. Does that philosophy make sense for Bond?
  • LEGO Batman is the fourth in the series. Who are you playing it with?
  • Both are licensed games that had every reason to be mediocre. What made the difference?
Conversation Starters
  • Licensed games failing is the rule. Licensed games succeeding is the exception. This week we got two exceptions in the same week.
  • Both of these games are single-player, story-driven experiences. No battle passes. Just a beginning, a middle, and an end.
To Move Things Along
  • Two for two this week. And we haven't even gotten to the racing game that's been sitting at the top of the charts...

Forza Horizon 6 — Out Now Pragmata — Out Now

Forza Horizon 6 has been out for ten days now and it hasn't left the top of the charts. Japan — the most requested setting in franchise history — delivered. Tokyo is five times larger than any previous urban area in the series.

Segment Three

What's Coming — Wolverine, GTA VI, and the Greatest Six Months in Recent Memory

Marvel's Wolverine — Sept 15 GTA VI — Nov 19
📅
Between now and the end of 2026: Marvel's Wolverine (Sept 15, PS5), GTA VI (Nov 19, PS5/Xbox), plus Gears of War: E-Day, Fable, and a new Halo still pencilled in for Xbox. This is not a normal second half of a year.

Marvel's Wolverine is five years in development, announced in 2021. Dark, visceral, brutal — deliberately unlike Spider-Man. Original story set across Tokyo, Canada, and Madripoor. This is the first entry in a planned X-Men trilogy.

And then there's GTA VI. The first Grand Theft Auto in thirteen years. Set in Leonida — modern-day Vice City. For elder gamers, returning to Vice City in 2026 is genuinely emotional. GTA Vice City came out in 2002. Lead with that on the panel.

💬 Host note: Let this one breathe. Give the panel time to actually talk about what GTA VI and Wolverine mean to them personally — not just as consumers, but as people who've been gaming for decades.

★ Panel Questions — Segment 3
  • Wolverine has been in development since 2021. What are you most nervous about, and what are you most excited about?
  • The X-Men trilogy model mirrors what Insomniac did with Spider-Man. Does that give you confidence or concern?
  • GTA Vice City came out in 2002. What does it mean to return to that world as an adult in 2026?
  • Analyst projections have GTA VI at a billion dollars in the first week. Does scale like that change how you approach a game?
Conversation Starters
  • Wolverine is not Spider-Man. Insomniac have been very deliberate about saying that. What does a dark Wolverine game actually look like in 2026?
  • The last GTA was 2013. A lot has happened in thirteen years — in gaming, in the world, in all of our lives. This game is going to land differently for people our age.
